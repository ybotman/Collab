## 0. The architecture decision (the load-bearing one)
**HDTS LLC is what you do. Halfway Down the Stairs is why you do it.**

| | **HDTSLLC.com** | **HalfwayDownTheStairs.com** |
|---|---|---|
| role | the **what** | the **why** |
| holds | services, portals, client work, agents, operations | philosophy, stories, essays, videos, speaking, the human side of AI |
| voice | credible, concrete, capable | thoughtful, human, curious |
| visitor leaves thinking | "these people can actually do it" | "I've never thought about it that way before" |

The *why* site is the moat. Anyone can list AI services. Almost no one earns the sentence *"I've never thought about it that way."* That sentence is the brand.

## 1. Brand Manifesto

> *There's a stair where I sit. There isn't any other stair quite like it.* — A.A. Milne

Most companies are obsessed with the top of the stairs — the destination, the deliverable, the "after."

We think the most important place is **halfway down.**

The transition. The in-between. The moment you've left where you were and haven't yet arrived where you're going — and you stop, and you think, and you change. The journey is where the change happens. The destination just collects it.

We are a company that lives in the middle of the staircase, and we help people ask the question almost no one stops to ask: **Why are we actually doing this?**

We've learned a few things sitting here:
- **Most requirements are misunderstood goals.** The man eating peas with honey isn't trying to enjoy peas — he's trying to keep them on the knife. Find the real objective and the strange solution makes sense.
- **Most processes are inherited constraints whose reason vanished long ago.** Nobody remembers the roasting pan was too small.
- **Asked to imagine the future, most people describe a faster horse.** Our work is helping them see the car.
- **Most organizations optimize the wrong metric.** They perfect the backup and forget the recovery.

So before we automate anything, we **simplify**; before we simplify, we **understand.** We never automate waste, bureaucracy, or confusion — that just makes the wrong thing faster.

And about AI: we are not, fundamentally, an AI company. We're a **people company** that believes AI is the most interesting tool of our lifetime. Humans are extraordinary at judgment, creativity, empathy, vision, and trust — and poor at tracking, repetition, context-switching, and coordinating across the walls we build between our own teams. **Agents belong in that gap** — not replacing people, but connecting them: crossing the silos, carrying the context, doing the work humans dislike so humans can do the work humans love.

The next decade isn't human-only. It isn't AI-only. It's **halfway down the stairs** — humans and agents learning to work together, in the place where contemplation and collaboration still matter.

That's where we live. Come sit on the stair.

## 2. Company Story (short form)
Halfway Down The Stairs is a consulting company for the in-between. We come into organizations at the moment of transition — the messy middle between "how we've always done it" and "what's now possible" — and we do the unglamorous, decisive work of asking *why* before anyone asks *how*. We strip requirements back to real objectives, retire inherited processes that no longer have a reason, and only then bring AI agents in — as connectors that help human teams work together better. We measure success not by how much we automated, but by how much friction we removed between people and the work they care about.

## 3. Founder Story (frame — to be told in Toby's voice)
The name is a poem the founder's father read to him as a child — *Halfway Down*, A.A. Milne. A small idea that never left: the stair that's "not at the bottom, not at the top" is "where I always stop." Decades later, building and fixing systems, the founder kept noticing the same thing everywhere — peas-and-honey requirements, pot-roast processes, faster-horse requests, backups that couldn't restore. The pattern under all of them: *people optimize the thing in front of them instead of the thing they actually want.* HDTS is the company built to sit on that middle stair with clients and ask the question the rest of the staircase skips. *(Founder to fill the lived detail — the actual moments, the father, the first time a "requirement" turned out to be an assumption.)*

## 4. Core Values (each is a story → a principle)
1. **The middle matters.** Transition over destination. *(the staircase)*
2. **Requirements are misunderstood goals.** Find the real objective. *(peas & honey)*
3. **Question every inherited process.** Does the original reason still exist? *(pot roast)*
4. **Help people imagine the car.** Not a faster horse. *(horse & carriage)*
5. **Optimize the real objective.** Not the convenient metric. *(the backups)*
6. **Understand → simplify → align → then automate.** Never automate waste. *(Elon's principle)*
7. **Agents connect; they don't replace.** Tear down silos, carry context. *(humans, teams, silos)*
8. **Technology should amplify humanity.** People do what they love; agents do the rest. *(human-AI philosophy)*
